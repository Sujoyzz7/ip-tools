Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 31, 2024

Our Commitment to Privacy

At IP Lookup Tool, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our services.

We believe in transparency and want you to understand exactly what data we collect and why. Our approach is simple: we collect only what we need to provide our services, and we never sell your data to third parties.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide

  • IP addresses you choose to lookup
  • Optional contact information if you reach out to us
  • Feedback and support requests

Automatically Collected Information

  • Browser type and version
  • Device information
  • IP address (for general location, not stored)
  • Usage patterns and analytics (anonymized)

Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Personal identification information (name, email, etc.)
  • IP lookup history
  • Location history
  • Files or documents from your device

How We Use Your Information

To Provide Our Services

Process IP lookup requests and display geolocation information

To Improve Our Services

Analyze usage patterns to enhance user experience

To Ensure Security

Monitor for abuse and maintain service integrity

Data Sharing and Third Parties

We Never Sell Your Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

Limited Sharing

We may share information only in the following circumstances:

  • With service providers who help us operate our service (e.g., hosting providers)
  • When required by law or to protect our rights
  • With your explicit consent

Cookies and Tracking

Essential Cookies

We use essential cookies to provide basic functionality and remember your preferences (like dark mode).

Analytics Cookies

We may use anonymized analytics to understand how our service is used and improve it.

No Tracking Cookies

We do not use tracking cookies for advertising or cross-site tracking.

Your Rights

GDPR Rights (EU Residents)

  • Right to access your data
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object

CCPA Rights (California Residents)

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt-out of sale of personal information

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

  • HTTPS encryption for all data transmission
  • Secure server infrastructure
  • Regular security audits
  • Limited data retention periods
  • Employee access controls
